Nutrition on Diabetes

Type 1 Diabetes

Proper nutrition in type 1 diabetes is vital for managing blood glucose levels and preventing complications. Since the body doesn't produce insulin, balancing the intake of carbohydrates, proteins, and fats helps regulate insulin doses and avoid blood sugar spikes. Key Points: Glucose Control: Managing carbs, especially with low glycemic index foods, ensures stable blood sugar levels. Preventing Complications: A balanced diet reduces the risk of heart disease, kidney damage, and nerve issues. Here are the nutrition options by age:

  • 0 to 5 years
  • 6 to 10 years
  • 11 to 19 years
  • 20 to 35 years
  • 36 to 60 years (LADA)

Type 2 Diabetes

Proper nutrition in type 2 diabetes is essential for managing blood glucose levels and preventing complications. Since insulin resistance is common, focusing on a balanced diet helps improve insulin sensitivity and control blood sugar. Key Points: Glucose Control: A diet rich in low glycemic index foods (whole grains, vegetables) helps stabilize blood sugar and avoid spikes. Preventing Complications: Healthy eating lowers the risk of heart disease, kidney problems, and nerve damage, common in type 2 diabetes. Here are the nutrition options by age:

  • 10 to 19 years
  • 20 to 45 years
  • 46 to 65 years
  • Over 65 years
